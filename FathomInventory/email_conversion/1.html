Meeting Summary ✨

Meeting Purpose

Explore biodiversity monitoring solutions for EcoRestoration Alliance's project in Panama

Key Takeaways

  • Everyone Counts offers a comprehensive biodiversity monitoring platform with sensors, data aggregation, and analysis

  • Platform includes camera traps, acoustic sensors, and soil analysis, with potential for additional environmental sensors

  • Pricing estimate: ~800€/month for 5 hotspots in France; custom pricing needed for Panama

  • EcoRestoration Alliance aims to demonstrate ecosystem restoration benefits, particularly for climate change mitigation

Topics

Everyone Counts Platform Overview

  • Aggregates biodiversity data from open sources, on-ground practices, and sensor networks

  • Provides detailed mapping, species identification, and ecological index calculations

  • Offers customizable dashboards for tracking biodiversity metrics over time

  • Currently used in various ecosystems, including vineyards, industrial areas, and hotels

Sensor Technology and Data Collection

  • Camera traps: Solar-powered, require SD card collection every 3 months

  • Acoustic sensors: Battery-powered (Wildlife Acoustics), need battery change every 3 months

  • Soil analysis: Conducted through subcontractors

  • Potential for additional environmental sensors (e.g., temperature, humidity) through partners like Brad

Data Analysis and Visualization

  • Calculates ecological indices (Shannon, Simpson, Specific Richness, Pirou)

  • Provides impact analysis of environmental factors on species

  • Allows comparison between different ecosystems and time periods

  • Offers species-specific analysis for endangered or important indicator species

Implementation Considerations

  • Typically 2-3 sensor hotspots recommended for 1 hectare

  • Challenges with customs and importation of equipment in some countries

  • Need for on-ground personnel to collect data and maintain equipment

  • Potential integration with existing open data sources for broader context

EcoRestoration Alliance Project Context

  • Focused on 1-hectare plot in Panama, transitioning from monoculture to regenerative agriculture

  • Aims to demonstrate environmental benefits and impact of restoration efforts

  • Seeks to build compelling narrative for scaling up restoration projects globally

Next Steps

  • Provide access to demo version of the platform for EcoRestoration Alliance

  • Explore available open data sources for the Panama site

  • Consider presentation of Everyone Counts solution to broader EcoRestoration Alliance network

  • Investigate potential logistical solutions for equipment deployment in Panama
